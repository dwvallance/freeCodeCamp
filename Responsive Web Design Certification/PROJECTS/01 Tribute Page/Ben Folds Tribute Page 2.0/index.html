The Life, Music and Achievements of Benjamin Folds

The Early Years

  • 1966 Born 'Benjamin Scott Folds' on September 12 in Winston-Salem, North Carolina.
  • 1984 Attends University of Miami's Frost School of Music on a full percussion scholarship for a term before losing his scholarship and dropping out.
  • 1985 Attends University of North Carolina - Greensboro
  • 1987 Marries childhood friend, Anna Goodman. The pair divorced in 1992.
  • 1988 Forms the band Majosha as bassist with friends Evan Olson and Millard Powers.
  • 1990 Moves to Nashville to pursue a music publishing deal with music executive Scott Siman.
  • 1993 Moves to Montclair, New Jersey where he begins acting in theater troupes and plays weekly gigs at the Sin-é café.

The Ben Folds Five Years

  • 1994 Forms Ben Folds Five as singer and pianist with bassist Robert Sledge and drummer Darren Jessee.
  • 1995 The group releases their debut self-titled album. Singles from the album include Underground which reached the Top 40 in the UK, and finished third in Australia's annual Triple J Hottest 100 countdown for 1996.
  • 1996 Marries his second wife, Kate Rosen. They divorced in 1997.
  • 1997 The group releases their second album titled Whatever and Ever, Amen, which reaches the top 10 in Australia and Japan. It contains their most successful single, Brick.
  • 1998 The group releases Naked Baby Photos, a compilation album of unreleased rarities, outtakes and live recordings from their first two albums.
  • 1999 The group releases their third studio album, The Unauthorised Biography of Reinhold Messner. The album's title refers to the name used by Darren Jessee and his friends on their fake IDs as teenagers; the band were unaware of the existence of the real Reinhold Messner, the first person to climb Mount Everest solo. That year, Ben Folds married his third wife, Frally Hynes, who he had two children with, twins Gracie (inspiring his song Gracie) and Louis (inspiring his song Still Fighting It. Hynes and Folds divorced in 2006.
  • 2000 The band amicably breaks up whilst working on a fourth studio album.

The Solo Years

  • 2001 Ben Folds releases his first solo album, Rockin' The Suburbs on which he plays almost every instrument.
  • 2002 Releases Ben Folds Live, a collection of live solo recordings.
  • 2003 Releases a pair of solo EPs, titled Speed Graphic and Sunny 16.
  • 2004 Releases a third solo EP, titled Super D.
  • 2005 Releases his second solo studio album, Songs for Silverman which reached Number 13 in the Billboard 200.
  • 2006 Releases Supersunnyspeedgraphic, the LP, a compilation of the songs that were on his three earlier EPs. On the same day as the release, Folds became the first person to webcast a live concert over MySpace, with the performance later released as a DVD.
  • 2007 Marries his fourth wife, Fleur Stanbrook. They ended their marriage in 2011, with Folds stating "As much as I love the idea of being married, it's not for me."
  • 2008 Releases his third solo studio album, Way To Normal, which became Folds' then highest-charting album ever, reaching Number 11 on the Billboard 200.
  • 2009 Releases an album consisting of arrangements of his music performed by some of the best college a capella groups in the US, titled Ben Folds Presents: University A Capella!. Later that year, he would feature as a judge on NBC's a capella competition The Sing-Off, a role he would continue to have for four seasons.
  • 2010 Releases a collaboration album with English novelist Nick Hornby, featuring music by Folds and lyrics by Hornby.

Recent Years

  • 2011 Ben Folds Five reunites to record three tracks for a Ben Folds retrospective titled The Best Imitation of Myself. Later that year, Ben Folds was inducted into the North Carolina Music Hall of Fame.
  • 2012 The group releases its first studio album in over a decade with the release of The Sound of the Life of the Mind, which debuted at Number 10 on the Billboard 200, making it Ben Folds' highest-charting album and the first to reach the top 10.
  • 2017 Ben Folds begins serving as the first artistic advisor to the National Symphony Orchestra at the Kennedy Center in Washington, D.C.
  • 2018 Ben Folds receives a star on the Music City Walk of Fame in Nashville, Tennessee
  • 2019 Releases his first book, a memoir titled A Dream About Lightning Bugs: A Life of Music and Cheap Lessons which became a New York Times bestseller. Also marries his current wife, Emma Sandall.
  • 2020 Releases the song 2020 which he recorded in Sydney, Australia where he has been living due to COVID-19 travel restrictions preventing him from returning home.
